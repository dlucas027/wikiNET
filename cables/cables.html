Fiber Optic Cables

Fiber optic cables use light signals to transmit data at high speeds over long distances.

  • Types: Single-mode (SMF) and Multi-mode (MMF)
  • High bandwidth and low latency
  • Immune to electromagnetic interference

Twisted Pair Cables

Twisted pair cables consist of copper wires twisted together to reduce interference.

  • Types: Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) and Shielded Twisted Pair (STP)
  • Commonly used in Ethernet networks
  • Cost-effective but limited in distance

Ethernet Cables

Ethernet cables are twisted pair cables specifically designed for wired network connections.

  • Categories: Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6a, Cat7, and Cat8
  • Higher categories support faster speeds and reduced crosstalk
  • Used in LAN and data center connections

Use Cases

Different cable types are suited for specific networking needs.

  • Fiber Optic: Long-distance and high-speed internet connections
  • Twisted Pair: Home and office networking
  • Ethernet: Data centers and enterprise networks
