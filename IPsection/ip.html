What is an IP Address?

An IP address is a unique identifier for a device on a network. It allows devices to communicate with each other over the internet or within a local network.

Public IP Address

A public IP is accessible from the internet and uniquely identifies a device across the entire web. It's assigned by your Internet Service Provider (ISP) and is used for online communication.

  • Assigned by ISPs
  • Used for internet communication
  • Unique across the globe

Private IP Address

Private IPs are used within local networks (LANs) to identify devices like computers, smartphones, and printers. They cannot be accessed directly from the internet.

  • Used in internal networks
  • Not routable on the internet
  • Assigned by routers
