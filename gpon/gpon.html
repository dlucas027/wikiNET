What is GPON?

GPON (Gigabit Passive Optical Network) is a fiber-optic technology that delivers high-speed internet using a passive optical network (PON) architecture. It supports higher data rates compared to EPON.

  • Uses passive optical splitters
  • Supports asymmetric data transmission
  • Commonly used for FTTH (Fiber-to-the-Home) services

How Does GPON Work?

GPON relies on Optical Line Terminals (OLT) and Optical Network Terminals (ONT) to transmit data efficiently over fiber-optic cables.

  • OLT sends and manages downstream traffic
  • ONT receives and distributes the data to end-users
  • Passive splitters distribute the signal to multiple users

GPON vs EPON

GPON and EPON are both PON technologies, but GPON provides higher efficiency in large-scale deployments with its advanced protocol stack and higher speeds.

  • GPON: Uses ATM, GEM, and TDM for data transmission
  • EPON: Purely Ethernet-based and simpler
  • GPON supports higher downstream speeds, making it ideal for ISPs

Advantages of GPON

GPON is preferred for its scalability and ability to support multiple services, such as internet, voice, and IPTV, over a single fiber connection.

  • Higher bandwidth capacity
  • Supports multiple services over the same network
  • More efficient data transmission than EPON

Where is GPON Used?

GPON is widely deployed in residential broadband, corporate networks, and ISP infrastructures due to its high efficiency and reliability.
